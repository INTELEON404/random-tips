How bug bounty platforms work

    The speaker explains that bug bounties are paid programs where companies reward hackers for finding vulnerabilities in their applications. Platforms like HackerOne, Bugcrowd, Synack, and Integrity serve as intermediaries, facilitating the process of signing up, reporting bugs, and receiving payments. Rewards can vary widely from $50 to $50,000, but sometimes hackers may not get paid at all. The speaker emphasizes the importance of starting with foundational steps before engaging with these platforms.

Recommended book for beginners

    The speaker recommends the book 'Realworld Bug Hunting' by Peter Yorski as an excellent resource for bug bounty hunters. Unlike outdated or overly theoretical guides, this book provides practical insights through real bug bounty reports, including detailed write-ups, payout amounts, and hacker reasoning. It presents the material in an engaging, story-like format and organizes bugs by categories such as authentication and logic.

Tools are assistants, not solutions

    The speaker emphasizes that tools in security testing are merely assistants that help identify potential issues, but they do not find bugs on their own. Success depends on the tester's skill to analyze and interpret the server's responses, requiring patience, intuition, and careful investigation rather than blind reliance on automation.

Patience and persistence in bug hunting

    The speaker emphasizes that becoming skilled requires obsession and accepting failure. Spending days without results, receiving unimportant feedback, and making mistakes are all part of the learning process. Each failed attempt teaches a lesson and each negative experience provides feedback. Despite moments of doubt, persistence brings you closer to success. The moment you successfully report a vulnerability and receive confirmation, you understand the purpose and reward of the effort.
